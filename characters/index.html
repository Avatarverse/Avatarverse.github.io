Characters

Creating a character

To create a character, do /create and finish the menu that opens. You need to fill out every section, then press the egg that says "Finalize." The lore on that egg will show you any field you're missing.

Deleting your character

To delete your character, do /delete. There is a 30-day cooldown to deleting your character.

Rolling for subelements

When you create a character, you are randomly assigned subelements (or affinities), such as healing or metalbending, based on the element you've chosen. Subelements have unequal chances of assignment:

Subelement Assignment chance
Icebending 100%
Plantbending 100%
Healing 45%
Sandbending 100%
Glassbending 80%
Metalbending 70%
Lavabending 10%
Lightningbending 55%
Combustionbending 10%
Spiritual affinity 40%
Flight 5%

Rerolling

You may find that you don't like the subelements your character has been given. The good news is that new characters have 2 free rerolls, where they can try again for different subelements, by using /reroll. And to see how many rerolls you have left, use /rerolls. Once you run out of these, if you want to reroll you will have to purchase a $3 reroll or the $20 Patron rank for infinite rerolls forever.

Character death

Ways to die:

Murdered

Your character can be murdered if you're successfully hunted. Click here to read more about hunts.

Executed

Your character can be executed using an item called the Executioner's Axe. This is in special circumstances when the Rules of War dictate someone's execution during a forced victory. Only admins may spawn the Executioner's Axe.

Slain in battle

Your character can be slain in battle. If you get knocked out in battle, someone will have 30 seconds to right-click on your body and choose to land the final blow (deleting your character) or spare you. Once those 30 seconds are up, you will be free to leave the battlefield with /battle leave or quit the game. Be warned, though: if you log off before those 30 seconds are done, your character will be killed automatically.

Duel to the Death

Duels to the Death (DttDs) are a special type of duel. When you get knocked out in a DttD, the winner of the duel will have the option to land the final blow or spare you. Read more about DttDs here.
